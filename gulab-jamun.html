Gulab Jamun

Ingredients

Khoya (mawa), sugar.

Speciality

Soft, juicy, and aromatic balls soaked in fragrant sugar syrup.

Origin

India, traditionally served during festivals, weddings, and celebrations.

History Behind the Name

“Gulab” means rose (referring to the syrup) and “Jamun” is a local fruit similar in shape and size. Hence the sweet is called Gulab Jamun.

What Makes Latkar Sweets Special?

We use slow-cooked khoya,pollish sugar and hand-rolled balls to get perfect softness and sweetness.

Our Thoughts

Each Gulab Jamun is a bite of celebration, perfect for family gatherings or gifting during festivals.
